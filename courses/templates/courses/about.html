What is Peergrading?

Peer Grading, commonly referred to as peer assessment or peer evaluation, is the grading process that allow students to judge the work of other students. It is a process whereby students or their peers grade assignments or tests based on a teacher’s benchmarks. The practice is employed to save teachers time and improve students' understanding of course materials as well as improve their metacognitive skills. The goal is for students to critically examine the academic work of others and evaluate its quality in order to practice and deepen their knowledge of academic work.

PeerGradeReview allows students to check and assess the work of their peers.

Why Peergrading?

Motivation
Being judged by peers is one of the most powerful sources of motivation in a classroom.
